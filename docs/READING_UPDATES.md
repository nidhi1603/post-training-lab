## Replication targets (verified 2026-07-14 against papers' own tables)

### Repair-R1 (2507.22853) — the blueprint for Phase 5, with a big asterisk

Their results on **our exact model** (Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B-Instruct), vanilla → RL-Both:

| Their benchmark | Before | After | Gain |
|---|---|---|---|
| HumanEval-repair | 33.04% | 81.25% | +48.21 |
| MBPP-repair | 23.74% | 66.15% | +42.41 |
| CodeForces-repair | 4.79% | 46.67% | +41.88 |
| CodeContests-repair | 5.45% | 39.85% | +34.40 |

- Training set: **only 5,421 samples, generated by mutating correct code** — independent
  convergence with our Amendment A1 pipeline. RL at 1.5B on mutation data demonstrably
  yields double-digit gains → Phase 5 is de-risked.
- **The asterisk:** their train AND test sets are mutations of the same four benchmarks
  (including HumanEval). Near-perfect train/test distribution match — under our ground
  rule 2 this is contamination-adjacent. Their +48 is in-distribution learning, not what
  they'd score on OctoPack's human-authored HumanEvalFix bugs.
- **Our differentiation (paper framing):** replicate their training regime on legal data
  (mutations of MBPP+/CommitPackFT, never HumanEval), evaluate strictly out-of-distribution
  on HumanEvalFix. Honest expected gain: +10–20 pts, not +48.
- **Calibration:** their 1.5B vanilla repair baseline = 33% ≈ OctoCoder's 30.4% — expect
  our Phase-1 baseline near/above the OctoCoder target → the decision gate likely
  retargets to the GPT-4 row (~47%).
- **TODO before Phase 5:** read their GitHub repo for exact hyperparameters (group size,
  LR, steps, rollout temperature) — replicating known-good hyperparameters is free insurance.

### rStar-Coder (2505.21297) — replicate the idea, not the scale

17.4→57.3% LiveCodeBench (7B) via 418K problems + 580K reasoning traces — two orders of
magnitude past our budget. **What we take:** the *mutual-verification mechanism* for
labeling test outputs without ground truth (multiple solutions vote on expected outputs)
→ Phase 2 test generation.

### AceCoder (2502.01718) — one extra filter

Synthesized tests kept only when pass-rates across sampled programs are consistent.
Cheap additional filter on top of the gold-sanity gate → Phase 2.

### CURE (2506.03136, NeurIPS Spotlight) — future work, not this project

Coder/tester co-evolution without ground-truth code. Smallest model 4B; adopting it
would change the GRPO arm's method mid-study. Name it in future work alongside
Repair-R1's test-first co-training.

## The three-question checklist before believing any "amazing result"

1. Is the test set truly **out-of-distribution** from training?
2. Is it **Qwen-only**? (Spurious Rewards 2506.10947 — our Llama control exists for this)
3. Were **decode settings matched** before/after?

Repair-R1 fails or is unclear on #1 and #2. Our design fixes both. That is the story of
our paper.
